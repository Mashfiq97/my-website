About Me

Hi, I'm Mashfiq Shahriar Zaman, a passionate graduate student in Computing Science at the University of Alberta, specializing in machine learning and data analysis.

My professional journey combines academia and industry, providing a diverse mix of experiences that shape my ideas and passion. I began my career as an associate software developer at IQVIA, a Fortune 500 company, where I implemented innovative solutions and automated workflows for processing monthly sales data efficiently for IQVIA's widely recognized application, Orchestrated Analytics, which is used by the largest pharmaceutical companies worldwide. Later, as a contractual lecturer at BRAC University, one of the top-ranked universities in Bangladesh, I had the opportunity to teach and mentor students in subjects such as data structures and software development.

Currently, I work as a graduate research assistant at the University of Alberta, one of the top-ranked universities in Canada, delving deeply into cutting-edge domains such as chip design optimization with deep reinforcement learning and preterm birth prediction in low-data settings. My contributions have resulted in significant publications in prestigious journals.

Beyond my professional life, I have a deep appreciation for nature and enjoy hiking, exploring scenic trails, and finding inspiration in the outdoors. Whether it is brainstorming innovative solutions, conquering a challenging hike, or engaging in meaningful collaborations, I thrive on pushing boundaries. With a strong portfolio of projects, academic achievements, and certifications, I am eager to impact the future of technology with curiosity, precision, and purpose.
