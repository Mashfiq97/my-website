About Me

Hi, I'm Mashfiq Shahriar Zaman, a passionate and driven graduate student in Computing Science at the University of Alberta who specializes in machine learning and data analysis.

My professional journey combines academia and industry, providing a diverse mix of experiences that shape my ideas and passion.

I started my career as a software developer at IQVIA, a Fortune 500 company, creating innovative solutions and automating workflows for multinational pharmaceutical companies.

Later, as a contractual lecturer at BRAC University, one of the top-ranked universities in Bangladesh, I had the opportunity to teach and mentor students in subjects such as data structures and software development.

Currently, I work as a graduate research assistant at the University of Alberta, one of the top-ranked universities in Canada, delving deeply into cutting-edge domains such as chip design optimization with reinforcement learning and preterm birth prediction in low-data settings. My contributions have resulted in significant publications in prestigious journals, demonstrating my commitment to advancing science and technology.

Beyond my professional roles, I enjoy solving complex problems, pushing the boundaries of innovation, and making a meaningful difference through research and collaboration. With a strong portfolio of projects, academic achievements, and certifications, I am eager to impact the future of technology with curiosity, precision, and purpose.
